Concept Exploration Brief — Confidential
Ecrof Media Co.
The Greens

Platform infrastructure for East Side Golf. A community network, course directory, membership program, and revenue architecture that turns the brand into the operating layer for Black golf in America.

01 / The Anchor
The original Green Book mapped where Black Americans were safe to go. The Greens maps where Black golfers are welcomed, respected, and valued — making it worth the trip on multiple levels.
The Negro Motorist Green Book — Victor Hugo Green, 1936–1966

This is not a marketing parallel. Augusta National did not admit a Black member until 1990. Country clubs excluded Black golfers for generations. Golf has a documented history of exclusion. East Side Golf exists as a direct response to that history. The Greens extends that response into permanent infrastructure that the community owns, maintains, and builds parallel to the very legacy of the game itself.

Golf greens. The Green Book. The Greens. The name carries the history, the mission, and the game in three syllables.

02 / Why This Moment

Community is the new currency.
Golf has always known that.

Tools and systems are handling more of what people do for work and for meaning. The pressure is not just job displacement. It is identity displacement. When systems can replicate most of what defined you professionally, the question becomes: what is irreducibly human?

The answer, historically and now, is presence. Shared experience. Trust built over time in physical space with other people. The brands and platforms that win in this environment are not the ones that add more technology to people's lives. They are the ones that use technology to create more reasons for people to show up for each other.

When systems can replicate almost any deliverable, the thing they cannot replicate is standing on a first tee at sunrise with three people you trust. That experience is the product. The Greens is the infrastructure that delivers it.

Why golf specifically

No other sport does what golf does. A round is four to five hours of sustained, in-person engagement with three other people. No screens, no interruptions, no performance metrics being monitored in real time. Business relationships deepened on a golf course convert at higher rates than any digital channel because the environment forces full presence. Deals are made. Partnerships are formed. Mentorships begin.

And unlike every other competitive sport, golf does not end when your body does. Basketball gives you until your thirties. Football less. Golf gives you a lifetime. The bond you build on a course at twenty-five can still be active and alive on that same course at seventy-five. Golf is a lifetime relationship instrument disguised as a sport. No other game can make that claim.

Connection

4–5 Hours of Presence

No other sport forces sustained, distraction-free engagement with the same three people for that long. Every round is a relationship deepened. Every round played together is a memory that compounds over years.

Longevity

A Lifetime Sport

You can play golf until you can no longer walk. The bonds formed on a course at twenty-five are still active at seventy-five. No other competitive sport offers that lifecycle. Golf is not a phase. It is a permanent relationship network.

Capital

Where Deals Get Made

Golf courses have always been where business relationships are sealed, not started. The informality of the walk, the shared frustration of a bad hole, the celebration of a good one. Trust is built in those moments faster than any boardroom allows.

Everywhere you look, platforms are building communities curated by your experiences, preferences, and goals. That is supposed to feel new. Golf has been doing it since the 15th century — building communities of practice, shared language, and mutual respect around the same walk, the same rules, and the same pursuit of a game that demands both humility and patience. The Greens is not inventing that community. It is building the infrastructure that makes it permanent, portable, and accessible to everyone who should have been part of it all along.

The structural inequity The Greens corrects

Black professionals have been systematically excluded from those relationship-building rounds for generations. Not just from the courses themselves, but from the network of deals, partnerships, and bonds that get formed on them. The economic consequence of that exclusion is real and long-running. East Side Golf exists as a cultural correction. The Greens operationalizes that correction at scale.

The Greens is not a diversity initiative. It is a community bond infrastructure built on top of the oldest and most durable relationship-building ritual in American sport and business. When community is the new currency, this platform is a long-duration asset.

03 / The Inflection Point

The brand is built.
The platform is the next move.

Olajuwon Ajanaku and Earl Cooper took East Side Golf from a T-shirt concept to $4M in annual revenue, a $3.4M raise from EP Golf Ventures (a PGA of America partnership), nine Jordan Brand collab shoes, a Mercedes partnership, and a Hulu documentary. The cultural credibility is established.

The business is still a product business. Every dollar traces back to apparel moving through a Shopify store. The community is real but it lives on Instagram, not inside a platform East Side Golf owns. The platform move is the right next step. The question is whether they design it correctly before committing capital to building the wrong version.

Most brands at this inflection point make the same mistake: they hire a technical team, start building an app, and spend six months on the product before they have designed how the platform should think. The Greens is designed architecture-first.

04 / Platform Architecture

Four pillars. One flywheel.

Each layer feeds the others. Removing any one weakens the whole structure.

Pillar 01

The Directory

Community-vetted course and driving range listings rated on welcome factor, price accessibility, skill-level fit, and overall vibe. Courses earn Greens certification. That certification drives member traffic. Member traffic is why courses want the badge.

Pillar 02

Community + Score Tracking

Members track rounds and build handicaps inside the platform. When they travel to any city, they open The Greens and see which courses are certified and which ESG members are local and available. Social infrastructure owned by East Side Golf, not borrowed from Instagram.

Pillar 03

The Membership

Annual access at $49.99. Monthly at $4.99. Unlocks discounted tee times and range access at affiliated courses nationwide, priority access to ESG drops and collabs, HBCU tournament access, and the full Greens directory. Every round logged at a certified course earns credit that offsets the membership fee — 9, 18, 27, or 36 holes each carry a defined dollar value. The more a member plays, the less they pay. Priced like a benefit with a built-in reward for showing up.

Pillar 04

The Referral Network

Members earn $5 via CashApp, Venmo, or Zelle for every new member they bring in. Members who refer a course that earns Greens certification earn $25–$50 plus a standing discount at that course. The network builds itself because the network is compensated for building it.

Technology layer: 18 Birdies has 9 million users, GPS on 43,000 courses, handicap tracking, and a social feed. A co-branded partnership (similar to their GolfNow tee time integration) gives East Side Golf fast access to that infrastructure while The Greens community layer and directory are built as owned assets. The partnership proves the concept. The owned platform is the destination.

05 / Rankings and Recognition

Status inside the community
is what keeps people engaged.

Strava has segments and KOMs. LinkedIn has endorsements. The Greens has a living ranking system that tracks performance, progress, travel, and relationship-building simultaneously. Every dimension of being an engaged golfer has a score. Every score has a leaderboard.

Rankings

Performance

National and regional leaderboards by handicap, score improvement, and consistency. Broken out by age group and skill tier so every member competes against a relevant field.

Rankings

Most Traveled

Ranked by number of certified courses played, cities visited, and states covered. Rewards members who bring the network to new markets. Every round they log in a new city expands the directory's credibility.

Rankings

Most Connected

Ranked by members referred, new relationships established, and playing partners connected across cities. Turns community-building into a tracked, recognized activity with real standing in the network.

Annual awards at the end of each season:

Awards

Most Improved

Biggest handicap drop over the season. Recognizes commitment to the game over raw talent. The most democratic award in the system.

Awards

Most Consistent

Strongest performance relative to handicap across the most rounds. Rewards showing up, not just peaking.

Awards

Road Warrior

Most courses visited across the most markets. The traveling member who expands the network just by playing.

Awards

Connector of the Year

Most new members brought in, most playing partners matched, most relationships built across the community.

Award recipients receive exclusive ESG merch drops, event access upgrades, course credits, and community recognition tied to Jordan Brand and sponsor activations. Recognition is the retention engine. Members who appear on leaderboards do not cancel memberships.

05b / The Play Credit

Every round at a certified course
pays toward the next membership period.

When a member plays at a certified course, The Greens collects a 3% affiliate fee from the course on that transaction. A defined portion of that fee flows back to the member as a direct credit against their membership cost — not points that expire, not a discount they have to remember to redeem. A real dollar reduction applied automatically at the next billing period.

9 Holes

$0.50 Credit

Executive courses, par-3s, half-rounds. The shortest play session still earns. Low barrier to first credit. Entry point for new or casual members.

18 Holes

$1.00 Credit

Standard round. The most common member session. Four rounds in a month covers 80% of the monthly membership fee from play alone.

27 Holes

$1.50 Credit

Back nine after a full round. Rewards the member who stays on course. Three sessions per month effectively wipes the monthly fee.

36 Holes

$2.50 Credit

Full-day golf. Highest reward tier. Two full-day outings at certified courses in a month covers the monthly fee entirely with credit left over.

The credit is self-funding. A member playing two 18-hole rounds at a certified course in a month generates $3.30 in affiliate revenue for The Greens and earns $2.00 in credit. The Greens retains $1.30 from those two rounds before the membership fee is collected. Every credit issued is already paid for by the activity that triggered it. The platform is not subsidizing engagement — it is sharing the affiliate revenue the engagement produced.

A member playing 18 holes four times a month at certified courses earns their full monthly membership fee back in credit. Their membership becomes complimentary through participation. The Greens still generates $6.60 in affiliate revenue from those four rounds before the credit is applied — netting $2.60 from affiliate activity alone on that member that month.

This is additive to the existing reward structure. A highly engaged member — playing regularly, referring new members, and referring qualifying courses — can receive more in total rewards than they pay in membership fees. The Greens still generates net positive revenue from every one of those activities. The member who costs the most in credits is also the member driving the most affiliate volume.

06 / The Collegiate Pipeline

Black collegiate golfers are the most natural members the platform will ever have.

They play constantly. They travel between home, their university, away matches, and summer courses across the country. They need exactly what The Greens provides: vetted courses in every city, playing partners in unfamiliar markets, and a community that reflects who they are.

Segment

HBCU Golfers

East Side Golf has already invested $300K+ in Morehouse's golf program and hosted HBCU tournaments. That relationship is the entry point. HBCU golf teams become founding member cohorts. Their coaches become Greens ambassadors on campus.

Segment

Black Golfers at PWIs

Black golfers on predominantly white institution teams are often isolated from community. The Greens gives them a network that travels with them. They are on courses in new cities every week. They become the fastest-growing source of directory submissions.

Student membership tier: $24.99 per year. Half price. The logic is not charity, it is pipeline. A student member who graduates becomes a full member. A full member who played 25 rounds per year as a student will play more as a professional with more income and more discretionary time. The lifetime value of a collegiate member acquired at $24.99 is multiples of a member acquired at $49.99 at age 35.

HBCU tournament hosting, already part of the ESG playbook, becomes a formal Greens event with registration, live scoring, rankings impact, and Jordan Brand sponsorship activation. Every tournament is a membership acquisition event.

07 / Why Courses Want This

Greens certification is not
a favor to East Side Golf.
It is a business decision.

Courses that earn certification are not doing East Side Golf a favor. They are gaining access to a pre-qualified, motivated, financially capable demographic that their existing marketing does not reach and that historically has had no structured pathway to their doors.

Course Gets

Warm Traffic

ESG members book rounds specifically at certified courses because of the discount and the badge. No cold acquisition. No ad spend. Members arrive pre-motivated and pre-qualified by the network.

Course Gets

The Jordan Brand Co-Sign

East Side Golf is Jordan Brand's only golf partner. Nine collab shoes. A shared apparel collection. That relationship does not trickle down to individual courses on its own. Greens certification changes that. Certified courses host Jordan-sponsored events, carry product they could not source independently, and carry the implicit endorsement of one of the most culturally powerful brands the world has ever produced. A small regional course cannot get a Jordan Brand meeting. The Greens gets them in the room by default.

Course Gets

Events and Outings

Scrambles, corporate outings, member-guest tournaments, golf retreats, family events, and Jordan Brand-sponsored activations. Courses that host these events see volume that no individual marketing effort generates. Golf is a family and community activity. The Greens activates that dimension at scale.

Course Gets

Pro Shop Placement

Certified courses carry East Side Golf product in the pro shop. Every golfer who walks through the door sees the brand. Courses benefit from the draw. ESG benefits from the surface area. The placement fee or wholesale margin compensates the course for the shelf space.

Jordan Brand. Mercedes-Benz. The PGA of America. The Arthur Blank Foundation. These are not relationships a regional golf course builds independently. They do not take those meetings. Greens certification makes a course part of a network that already has those relationships. The stamp of approval is not from East Side Golf alone. It is, by association, from every partner East Side Golf has built. That is a de facto endorsement no local marketing budget can manufacture.

Course certification terms: a 3% affiliate fee on green fees and range buckets paid by ESG members, preferential rack rates for members (15–20% off standard), and willingness to carry ESG product. In exchange: the badge, the traffic, the events, and access to the full brand ecosystem.

The renegotiation leverage

The 3% affiliate fee is the entry price. It is designed to make yes easy. Once the concept proves out and certified courses can point to measurable revenue lift from ESG member traffic, Jordan Brand event hosting, and pro shop volume, the conversation changes. East Side Golf is no longer asking for a favor. It is holding demonstrated value with data behind it. The affiliate rate moves up because the results support it, and it can happen almost immediately once the concept gains traction in a market.

The reinvestment loop runs parallel to that. Increased traffic and revenue gives course operators the capital to invest in better turf, better facilities, better staff, and a better overall experience. A better course attracts more golfers of all kinds, not just ESG members. That broader improvement is directly attributable to Greens certification even when the golfers arriving are not card-carrying members. The certification makes the course better. A better course generates more traffic. More traffic generates more of everything. That loop is the argument for why courses should not just accept certification but actively compete for it.

Certification is not a cost to the course. It is a growth mechanism. The 3% affiliate fee funds a revenue and reputation lift that compounds over time, and the rate East Side Golf commands grows with it.

Black golfers have grown 123% since 2020, driven by cultural brands and post-pandemic participation gains. Isolating for Black golfers specifically: using U.S. demographic share and participation rates, the addressable market is estimated at 2.5 million Black on-course golfers nationally, with a broader casual and off-course population of 4 million or more.

09 / Revenue Architecture

Seven streams. All connected.

R01

Membership Fees

$49.99/year standard. $24.99/year student. The base layer that funds everything else. Every member who joins increases the network value for every existing member.

R02

Green Fee and Range Affiliate (3%)

3% of every discounted transaction a member completes at an affiliated course. Paid by the course as the cost of warm customer acquisition. $29.70 per member per year from green fees alone. $10.80 from range visits. Scales directly with membership and engagement.

R03

Events, Scrambles, Retreats, Tournaments

$15 platform take per player per event. Average $3,000 in sponsorship per event from Jordan Brand, Mercedes, and ESG brand partners. 20 events in Year 1. 150 in Year 3. Hosted at certified courses, which drives their traffic and deepens the affiliate relationship. Golf is inherently social and familial. Every family scramble, corporate outing, and HBCU tournament runs through the platform.

R04

Pro Shop Wholesale

Certified courses carry East Side Golf product. Every golfer who walks through the door sees the brand. The pro shop channel puts ESG product in front of golfers who would never find it through Instagram. Average $2,000 in wholesale per course per year. 200 certified courses by Year 3 = $400,000 in wholesale channel revenue.

R05

Exclusive Member Drops

Members get first access to ESG drops and collabs. Some drops are members-only. Limited quantities tied to rankings and award recognition. The drop becomes a retention mechanism. Members who want access to the next Jordan collab keep their membership active. 20% of 20,000 members purchasing one drop item at $65 average generates $260,000 in platform-attributable merch lift by Year 3.

R06

Referral Network

$5 to the member who brings in a new member (CashApp / Venmo / Zelle). $25–$50 to the member who refers a course that earns certification, plus a standing discount at that course. The referral cost is the cheapest customer acquisition the platform will ever run. $1 in referral payouts returns roughly $90 in annual per-member value.

R07

Sponsorship and Brand Activation

Jordan Brand, Mercedes, Arthur Blank Foundation, and future ESG brand partners sponsor events, fund awards, and activate inside the platform directory. Rankings and award announcements are natural sponsorship inventory. The Connector of the Year award presented by Jordan Brand. The Road Warrior leaderboard presented by a travel partner. Every recognition moment is a sponsorship placement that does not feel like an ad.

R08

The Play Credit (Engagement-Funded Reward)

Members earn a direct credit against their membership fee for every round logged at a certified course — $0.50 for 9 holes, $1.00 for 18, $1.50 for 27, $2.50 for 36. The credit is not a cost center. It is funded entirely by the affiliate revenue the activity generates: The Greens collects 3% from the course on that same transaction. On every credit-earning round, the platform retains net positive affiliate revenue after paying the credit. The member who plays the most earns the most in credits and generates the most in affiliate volume. The reward and the revenue travel together. This is additive to the member referral reward ($5) and the course referral reward ($25–$50) — a member who plays regularly, refers new members, and refers qualifying courses can receive more in total rewards than they pay in membership fees while The Greens still runs net positive on their activity.
